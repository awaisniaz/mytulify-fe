Wrong aspect ratios get your video letterboxed, your Reel cropped, or your feed post looking soft. Designers and creators search for these sizes every day — memorize the big four and you’re covered.

## Platform cheat sheet

| Platform | Ratio | Common size |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| YouTube / landscape video | **16:9** | 1920×1080 |
| Reels / Shorts / TikTok | **9:16** | 1080×1920 |
| Instagram / Facebook square | **1:1** | 1080×1080 |
| Instagram portrait feed | **4:5** | 1080×1350 |

## How to calculate any size

If you know width and height in pixels, simplify with the greatest common divisor. Example: 1920÷120 and 1080÷120 → **16:9**.

Or open Mytulify’s [Aspect Ratio Calculator](/image-tools/aspect-ratio-calculator), paste dimensions (or tap a preset), and copy the CSS `aspect-ratio` snippet for your site.

## Workflow tip

1. Pick the platform ratio first.
2. Resize or crop the asset to match ([Resize Image](/image-tools/resize-image), [Crop Image](/image-tools/crop-image)).
3. Compress before upload so pages stay fast ([Compress Image](/image-tools/compress-image)).

Get the ratio right once — every platform crop after that becomes muscle memory.
