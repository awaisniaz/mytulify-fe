UTM parameters are the simplest way to know which campaigns send traffic to your site. Without them, paid ads, newsletters, and social posts blur together in Google Analytics 4.

## The five UTM fields

| Parameter | Purpose | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| `utm_source` | Where traffic comes from | `newsletter`, `facebook`, `google` |
| `utm_medium` | Channel type | `email`, `cpc`, `social` |
| `utm_campaign` | Promotion name | `summer_sale_2026` |
| `utm_term` | Paid keyword (optional) | `online+tools` |
| `utm_content` | Creative variant (optional) | `header_cta` |

Keep names lowercase, use underscores, and never put spaces in values.

## How to create a link on Mytulify

1. Open the [UTM Builder](/seo-web-tools/utm-builder).
2. Paste your destination URL.
3. Fill source, medium, and campaign (add term/content if you A/B test ads).
4. Copy the generated URL into ads, emails, or social bios.

## Naming tips that save reporting pain

- Agree on a short glossary (`email` not `e-mail` / `Email`).
- Put the date or quarter in the campaign when it helps (`launch_q3`).
- Don’t reuse the same campaign name for unrelated promotions.

Clean UTMs turn GA4 into a decision tool instead of a mystery. Use the free builder anytime you ship a campaign link.
