Chasing payment or following up with silent clients is one of the most uncomfortable parts of freelancing. The right wording raises response rates without sounding pushy or desperate — and you do not need a paid CRM to send a clear email.

Below are five templates you can paste today. When you want names, amounts, and tone filled in automatically, use the free [Freelance Email Templates Generator](/freelancer-tools/email-templates-generator).

## 1. Friendly invoice reminder (first nudge)

**When to use it:** A few days after the due date, when you still assume the invoice slipped through. This is not a threat — it is a clear restatement of what is owed.

**Tip:** Always restate the original invoice amount and due date so there is no ambiguity. Attach or link the same invoice you sent from the [Invoice Generator](/converters-generators/invoice-generator).

```
Subject: Friendly reminder: invoice for [Project]

Hi [Client],

Hope you’re doing well! Quick nudge on the invoice for [Project].

Amount due: $[Amount]
Original due date: [Due date]

If you’ve already sent payment, thank you — please ignore this note. Otherwise, whenever you can take care of it this week would be appreciated.

I can resend the invoice or share updated payment details if helpful.

Thanks so much,
[Your name]
```

## 2. Firm invoice reminder (second notice)

**When to use it:** After a friendly reminder with no reply, or when the invoice is clearly overdue (for example 10–14+ days). Stay factual: days overdue, amount, and a concrete next step.

**Tip:** If your contract allows late fees, calculate them with the [Late Payment Fee Calculator](/freelancer-tools/late-fee-calculator) before you quote a new total — do not invent numbers in the email.

```
Subject: Overdue invoice for [Project] — action needed

Hello [Client],

Your invoice for [Project] is now [X] day(s) past due.

Amount due: $[Amount]
Original due date: [Due date]
Days overdue: [X]

Please arrange payment within the next 5 business days, or reply with a confirmed payment date. If there’s a billing issue on your side, tell me what you need and I’ll help unblock it.

Regards,
[Your name]
```

## 3. Project follow-up (proposal went quiet)

**When to use it:** Five to seven days after you sent a proposal and heard nothing. Keep it short; give them an easy “not now” exit so you can stop waiting.

**Tip:** Name the project in the subject line. Vague “just checking in” emails get archived.

```
Subject: Following up on my proposal for [Project]

Hi [Client],

I’m following up on the proposal I shared for [Project].

I’m still excited about helping and am holding a tentative start window. Happy to adjust scope or timing if that helps — or a quick “not now” is useful so I can plan.

Best regards,
[Your name]
```

## 4. Cold pitch / introduction

**When to use it:** When you are reaching out to a prospect who did not ask for a proposal yet. Lead with how you help, not your life story.

**Tip:** One ask only — a short call or permission to send a one-page outline. Pair a won pitch with a clear [Freelance Contract](/freelancer-tools/contract-generator) before work starts.

```
Subject: Introduction — help with [Project]

Dear [Client],

I’m a freelancer who helps teams like yours with [Project].

I specialize in delivering clear scope, timelines, and results — without the overhead of a big agency.

If this is relevant, I’m happy to share a brief proposal or jump on a quick call.

Best regards,
[Your name]
```

## 5. Scope change notification

**When to use it:** The moment a client asks for “one more thing” outside the agreed deliverables. Confirm cost before you start — unpaid extras destroy margins.

**Tip:** Document the change with a signed change order after they reply yes. Email alone is not enough for bigger additions.

```
Subject: Scope update for [Project] — confirmation needed

Hello [Client],

I’m confirming a scope change on [Project] before I proceed.

Proposed addition: work beyond the agreed deliverables.
Estimated additional fee: $[Amount]

Please reply with approval (or questions) so I can update the timeline and issue a change order / revised invoice. I won’t start the extra work until you confirm.

Regards,
[Your name]
```
